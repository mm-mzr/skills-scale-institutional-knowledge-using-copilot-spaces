# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management documentation. This folder contains the process guides, templates, and reference materials that define how OctoAcme plans and delivers work. Use this README as a starting point to navigate the docs and understand our overall approach.

---

## Overview of OctoAcme Project Management

OctoAcme operates with a structured yet iterative project management approach grounded in customer-first principles and data-informed decision-making. The organization follows a clear lifecycle spanning five phases: initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospective. Each phase is supported by lightweight but comprehensive artifacts—including project one-pagers, prioritized backlogs, risk registers, and retrospective notes—that ensure transparency and continuity. The initiation phase emphasizes stakeholder alignment and measurable success criteria through a one-pager template that captures the problem statement, objectives, success metrics, and resource requirements. Once approved at a decision gate, the organization moves into planning, where work is broken into shippable increments with clear acceptance criteria, dependencies are mapped, and a release timeline is established.

The organization operates with clearly defined roles and responsibilities across three primary personas: Project Managers who coordinate delivery schedules, manage risks and communications, and maintain transparency across stakeholders; Product Managers who define customer value, prioritize the roadmap, and measure outcomes through data-driven decisions; and Developers who implement features collaboratively while contributing to estimating, design, and identifying technical risks. This role clarity, combined with OctoAcme's principle of "clear ownership" where each project has a named Project Manager and Product Lead, ensures accountability while enabling cross-functional collaboration. The communication strategy is intentionally structured with a consistent cadence: daily standups focused on blockers and dependencies, weekly syncs between PM and Product Lead, twice-weekly delivery team standups, and monthly stakeholder updates.

Quality assurance and continuous improvement are embedded throughout OctoAcme's execution model rather than treated as afterthoughts. During execution, the team uses a project board workflow (such as GitHub Projects) with standardized columns, maintains a pull request discipline with small reviewable PRs, runs automated tests and security scanning in CI/CD pipelines, and conducts manual QA for feature acceptance when needed. This feedback loop, combined with metrics tracking (velocity, burndown, success metrics from the one-pager, and observability dashboards), enables OctoAcme to continuously refine its processes and deliver increasingly efficient results.
